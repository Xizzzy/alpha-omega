## Options Considered

### winner when unique

**Omega's view:**
Default to the winner brain when `winning_brain` is unambiguous (`alpha` or `omega`). If the session says `both` or `unknown`, stop and require `--executor` instead of silently biasing to one side.
- Pros: Matches the repo's symmetry goal: the brain whose approach won gets first implementation rights; Fits the existing session-ledger design and current `--executor winner` shape; Avoids the bad silent-alpha fallback when `winning_brain` is `both` or `unknown`; Easy to explain to users: debate winner implements unless the winner is ambiguous
- Cons: Winning the argument does not always mean being the most reliable executor; Requires one extra explicit choice in tie or ambiguity cases; Depends on `winning_brain` being computed correctly from the session artifact

### always explicit executor

**Omega's view:**
Make `ao implement` require `--executor alpha|omega` every time. This removes ambiguity and avoids incorrect defaults, but it throws away useful information already produced by Sigma and the session ledger.
- Pros: No silent wrong default; Very simple to implement and reason about; Lets users choose the stronger executor for a specific codebase or task
- Cons: Adds friction to every implementation run; Weakens the debate-to-implementation handoff AO is trying to automate; Feels less deterministic because the user must reinterpret the result

### default alpha fallback

**Omega's view:**
Default to `winner`, but when the winner is ambiguous, fall back to Alpha automatically. This is operationally simple and matches the current code, but it bakes in asymmetry without strong evidence.
- Pros: Zero extra user friction; Already implemented in the current CLI; Gives deterministic behavior even for `both` and `unknown`
- Cons: Violates the equal-agency story; Creates hidden product bias toward Alpha; Hard to defend if Omega won credibility in prior debates; Masks ambiguity instead of surfacing it

## Assumptions

- AO's product goal is symmetry between Alpha and Omega, not convenience for one wrapper
- Tie or ambiguity cases (`both`, `unknown`) are common enough that silent fallback matters
- Users can tolerate an explicit `--executor` only in ambiguous cases
- `winning_brain` is stored in session JSON and is usually reliable enough to drive defaults

## Blind Spots Acknowledged

- **Omega:** I am probably weighting protocol symmetry more than raw executor reliability. I may also be underestimating how often `winning_brain` becomes ambiguous, which would make the recommended behavior feel less convenient than it looks on paper.
